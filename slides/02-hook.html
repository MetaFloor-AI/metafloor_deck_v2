what a supply-chain decision actually is

2 a.m. An undersea cable snaps 400 km off California.

  • A region drops to 63% capacity — $50,000 an hour, ticking.
  • Someone must decide now: who has a spare? which ship is closest? what does the contract allow?
  • The answer already exists — scattered across 6 systems and 8 people's heads.
  • By hand, assembling it takes ~30 hours.
the punchline That GAP — 30 hours vs. minutes — is the product.
